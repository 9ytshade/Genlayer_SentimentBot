## 2. Make Smart Contracts Smart Again

In 1994, Nick Szabo proposed the concept of smart contracts, followed by Vitalik Buterin, who implemented them in a permissionless environment (Ethereum) in 2013. Regular contracts are incapable of self-enforcing their rules and are slow and expensive. Smart contracts were a great innovation that solved those problems, but they are isolated from the outside world and are limited to processing simple, predefined rules.

In a future where AGI has the lion’s share of the volume of commerce, a new mechanism is required that is capable of enabling self-enforcing smart contracts that can access real-world data and process natural language. We call them **Intelligent Contracts**.

## 7. Decentralize AI

AI has the potential to become the key pillar of societal advancement in the coming years. However, if we don’t decentralize it, we risk creating a world where AI’s power is concentrated. While decentralized training and inference are important, they are not enough to truly decentralize AI. Relying on a single AI model centralizes [decision-making, making the system vulnerable to biases, incorrect responses, and various attack vectors](https://www.genlayer.com/post/making-ai-on-the-blockchain-safe).

You can’t claim to have a trustless system if all your trust is placed in one single model. A single AI model used for decision-making can be compromised through prompt injection attacks or fail to perform consistently due to its probabilistic nature. This makes it unusable for many applications, especially in the high-stakes DeFi applications, where reliability is a must.  

![](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/6818c01e63135b5d665dc9df/6846edbdd2100cf06d5c5942_671a33cd7fc33aeccdd2dfa1_AD_4nXfBYxbdYk_nWl1OmbQ62SQQlKZ2Gp5_hvsYmgg1ae8QwrwuUzFf3GfI-lG3VR00IpYPw9q4JlkMzdpkBKO4-EEPWzeu6hiuv2Pp-3GLLZEybTsvPQpwVbj2xI5q60lRX-EYGI7YYln8K9L2hLe-WMmNqKW9.avif)

GenLayer decentralizes AI by distributing decision-making across multiple validators connected to different AI models, ensuring that no single model holds all the power. This makes GenLayer’s system resilient to biases, attacks, and errors. Decentralized AI is not just a technical necessity, it’s essential for fairness and transparency as we move toward an AI-governed future.

## 8. Make On-chain AI Safe and Reliable

[GenLayer is built for safety and reliability.](https://www.genlayer.com/post/making-ai-on-the-blockchain-safe) If a validator produces an incorrect or malicious output, other validators will not let it slide and will vote against it. Additionally, anyone can dispute a transaction, doubling the validator set and attempting to overturn previous results. This mechanism ensures that the decision-making is correct and safe.

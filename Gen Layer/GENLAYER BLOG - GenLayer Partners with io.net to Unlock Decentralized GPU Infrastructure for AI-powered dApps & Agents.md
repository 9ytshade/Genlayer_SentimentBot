# GenLayer Partners with io.net to Unlock Decentralized GPU Infrastructure for AI-powered dApps & Agents

**San Francisco, CA | July 1, 2025** – GenLayer, the trustless decision maker for the AI Age, today announced a strategic partnership with io.net, a decentralized cloud provider for on-demand GPU clusters. This collaboration merges GenLayer’s AI-native consensus with [io.net](http://io.net/)’s distributed compute network, establishing a foundation for decentralized, high-throughput inference for dApps and Agents. 

Through this partnership, GenLayer validators will gain access to over 30 open-source AI models running on thousands of geo-distributed GPUs, which will power AI-driven contract execution, autonomous dispute resolution, and real-world data processing.

"AI won't wait for lawyers. If we want AI Agents to participate in economic activities, we need infrastructure that operates at machine speed," said Albert Castellana, Co-Founder and CEO of GenLayer Labs. "We're using a blockchain that has multiple AIs coordinating and reaching agreement on subjective decisions, as if they were a judge. We're basically building a global synthetic jurisdiction that has an embedded court system that doesn't sleep, that's super cheap, and operates at machine speed."

## **Unlocking a New Class of AI-Powered Applications**

By combining io.net's decentralized GPU infrastructure with GenLayer's Intelligent Contracts, developers can now build applications with capabilities far beyond traditional smart contracts:

- **AI-Powered Commerce**: Power AI-to-AI transactions where agents can negotiate, settle, and resolve disputes without human intervention, scaling digital commerce to machine speed.
- **Dynamic Oracles**: Replace static data feeds with LLM-driven oracles capable of subjective reasoning and natural language interpretation, bringing nuanced decision-making on-chain, such as:   
    - **Real-Time Insurance Payouts**: Trigger settlements based on live event monitoring and web-sourced data, with AI validators interpreting conditions and executing payouts automatically.
    - **Prediction Markets:** Enable markets that resolve based on natural language outcomes, real-time events, and subjective conditions: validated trustlessly by decentralized AI.
- **Autonomous DAOs**: These enable decentralized organizations to adapt in real time to new information and make governance decisions based on real-world context.

## **Key Benefits of the Partnership**

The collaboration unlocks: 

- **Enhanced Model Diversity**: GenLayer validators gain access to up to 30 open-source models, including Llama and Deepseek, significantly improving consensus reliability and reducing single-point-of-failure risks.
- **Scalable Infrastructure**: GenLayer built dApps gains scalability, low latency, and enterprise-ready reliability.
- **Seamless Integration**: Builders can directly utilize decentralized AI inference integrated into GenLayer's Optimistic Democracy consensus through io.net's on demand GPU. 
- **Global Accessibility**: The partnership enables worldwide access to GenLayer's synthetic jurisdiction, allowing AI agents and organizations globally to leverage trustless arbitration and automated contract enforcement.

"GenLayer’s AI driven contracts, integrated with [io.net](http://io.net/)’s decentralized GPU infrastructure, will enable developers to build efficient, autonomous, and trusted AI applications. The partnership advances decentralized commerce," Tausif Ahmed, CBDO, [io.net](http://io.net/) said.

## **About io.net**

io.net is a decentralized cloud provider that deploys and manages on-demand, decentralized GPU clusters from geo-distributed sources. With thousands of GPUs accessible in the IO Network, the platform is specifically architected for low-latency, high-processing demand use cases like AI/ML operations. io.net also operates IO Intelligence, a free-inference platform providing AI developers access to up to 30 open-source models and tools for building agents using agentic infrastructure and RAG as a service. The company is focused on democratizing access to compute resources and enabling the next generation of AI-powered applications.

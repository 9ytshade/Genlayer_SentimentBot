## Building the Intelligence Layer of the Internet

[Blockchains suffer from an isolation issue:](https://www.genlayer.com/post/genlayer-the-intelligence-layer-of-the-internet) they’re disconnected from the Internet. The only way to introduce off-chain or real-world data into blockchains is through oracles, which are limited to existing data sources and add a degree of centralization. More than that Blockchains are also not capable of understanding natural language instructions as they can only deal with code. This limits their capabilities and is causing stagnation in the entire crypto ecosystem; most new dApps are forks or only incremental improvements. 

GenLayer presents a solution to those problems, [enabling Intelligent Contracts to use AI models](https://www.genlayer.com/post/what-is-genlayer-the-fundamentals-part-i) and access the Internet’s knowledge to make complex decisions in a decentralized manner. Additionally, Intelligent Contracts can process natural language instructions, and handle non-deterministic operations, enabling a whole new generation of smart contract logic that is not possible under the limitations of current blockchain infrastructure. 

The [GenLayer blockchain, composed of a Consensus Layer and a Virtual Machine](https://www.genlayer.com/post/what-is-genlayer-the-fundamentals-part-ii), leverages AI to achieve consensus on subjective tasks through a unique mechanism called Optimistic Democracy. The delegated Proof-of-Stake (dPOS) system involves a randomly selected subset of five validators, each connected to a different LLM. The leader proposes an output, which the other four validators compare against their own results, voting on whether to accept it. If a majority agrees, the transaction is processed; if not, a new leader is chosen. An appeal window follows validation, allowing participants to post a bond and double the validator set for a re-evaluation.
